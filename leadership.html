IU Imaga

BMLS, MPH, AMLSCN

Founder & Chairman, Imaga Empire

IU Imaga is a Medical Laboratory Scientist with an MPH in Epidemiology and a National Health Fellow, bridging laboratory diagnostics, public health systems, and community healthcare delivery in Nigeria.

With over four years of hands-on experience in molecular diagnostics, patient care, viral load testing, and Early Infant Diagnosis (EID), IU focuses on strengthening the critical links between robust lab systems and effective public health response.

In primary health centres, he has repeatedly observed the same challenge: high-quality laboratory data that fails to translate into timely action. Viral load results and EID often sit unused, supply chains stall, and patients are lost to follow-up — a gap he works tirelessly to close.

As Founder of Spark a Smile Initiative (now part of the Isaiah Imaga Foundation), IU leads medical outreach, education, and community support programs for underserved populations, turning policy and data into tangible impact on the ground.

Beyond the laboratory, he maintains a creative practice through his blog "A Writer's Lab," where he explores themes of connection, reflection, and the human stories behind the data. IU believes empathy and systems thinking must go hand in hand in public health.

IU is actively open to opportunities in public health, health systems strengthening, program implementation, laboratory leadership, and policy roles — particularly those that combine evidence, equity, and real-world execution. He can be reached at iu@imaga.group.
